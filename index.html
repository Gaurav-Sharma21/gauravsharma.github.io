Projects & Research Work

Physics-Informed Neural Network for Fatigue Life Prediction

Master’s Thesis – University of Freiburg (2026)

Developed a two-stage framework combining Gaussian Process Regression and Physics-Informed Neural Networks for fatigue life prediction under extreme data scarcity.

Introduced thermodynamic constraints including monotonicity, entropy-rate consistency, and fracture fatigue entropy invariance to enforce physically valid predictions.

Achieved R² up to 0.91 across multiple composite material datasets without retraining.

AI Research Engineer – University of Freiburg

Designed and deployed an end-to-end anomaly detection pipeline using thermal and ultrasonic sensor data for fatigue failure prediction.
